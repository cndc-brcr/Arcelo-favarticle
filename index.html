UNESCO’s first-ever
Goodwill Ambassador for Youth

Goodwill Ambassador for Youth

Global music powerhouse SEVENTEEN, recognized for having the best-selling album of 2023, is set to be appointed as UNESCO's inaugural Goodwill Ambassador for Youth. This prestigious designation will be conferred by UNESCO Director-General Audrey Azoulay during a ceremony on June 26 at the organization's headquarters in Paris.

"SEVENTEEN and UNESCO share a profound commitment to empowering young people to lead change and tackle contemporary global challenges," said Azoulay. She praised the group for inspiring millions with their music and energy and emphasized their growing partnership, which has already seen impactful initiatives. "I am thrilled to officially welcome the 13 members of SEVENTEEN into UNESCO’s Goodwill Ambassador family," she added.

Global Youth Grant Scheme

This appointment follows SEVENTEEN’s notable participation in the 13th UNESCO Youth Forum in November 2023, where the group championed youth solidarity and performed for representatives from UNESCO’s 194 member states and global youth delegates. Their earlier "#GoingTogether" campaign, launched in partnership with the Korean National Commission for UNESCO, supported education programs in Timor-Leste and Malawi, further demonstrating their dedication to youth causes.

Going Together - For Youth Creativity and Well-Being

UNESCO and SEVENTEEN Unite for Youth Creativity and Well-Being In June 2024, UNESCO and K-pop sensation SEVENTEEN launched an innovative global initiative, "Going Together – For Youth Creativity and Well-Being," aimed at empowering young people through creative and community-focused projects. Backed by a $1 million donation from SEVENTEEN, the program seeks to address challenges affecting youth development and well-being across diverse regions.

Source: UNESCO

At its core, the Going Together campaign is a rallying cry for young changemakers to address pressing societal issues through innovative projects. With grants ranging from $5,000 to $10,000, the initiative provides resources for youth-led endeavors in three key areas: building confidence and resilience through music, arts, and sports; creating vibrant, creative communities; and improving youth mental health and well-being. This multi-faceted approach aligns with UNESCO’s mandate and the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly those focused on education, equality, and climate action. During the ceremony, member Joshua expressed the group’s honor at representing youth, emphasizing their journey of growth and their bond with their fanbase, CARATs. The group aims to inspire young people to pursue their dreams while fostering supportive communities.

Looking Ahead: A Model for Future Initiatives

SEVENTEEN’s involvement in this campaign represents a transformative model of celebrity influence. Their global reach, combined with UNESCO’s institutional expertise, has amplified the campaign’s impact, making it accessible to diverse communities worldwide. This partnership highlights the role of public figures in galvanizing youth and underscores how creative industries can contribute meaningfully to global development goals.

The success of Going Together paves the way for future collaborations that leverage the cultural appeal of artists like SEVENTEEN to inspire and mobilize youth. By providing mentorship, training, and financial support, the campaign demonstrates how creative partnerships can empower young people to address local and global challenges. Moreover, the initiative fosters an enduring message: with the right resources and platforms, today’s youth can lead transformative social change.

As UNESCO and SEVENTEEN continue to champion creativity and well-being, *Going Together* stands as a shining example of how innovative partnerships can empower future generations. This campaign not only addresses immediate challenges but also lays a foundation for youth-centered programs that combine cultural influence with actionable solutions. With its scalable and inclusive framework, *Going Together* is not just an event but a movement—a guiding light for future initiatives aimed at inspiring young leaders around the world
